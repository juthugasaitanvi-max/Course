Mini Biography : Ratan Tata

Ratan Naval Tata (born 28 December 1937) is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and a former chairman of Tata Sons. He is the chairman emeritus of Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group, an Indian conglomerate. He is also the chairman of Tata Trusts, India's largest charitable trust, which owns two-thirds of the equity of Tata Sons.

Early Life

Ratan Tata was born in Mumbai, India, to Naval Tata and his wife, Sooni. He is the great-grandson of Jamsetji Tata, the founder of the Tata Group. He went on to study architecture at Cornell University in the United States.

Important Achievements

Ratan Tata has been instrumental in the growth and expansion of the Tata Group, overseeing the acquisition of several international companies, including Tetley, Jaguar Land Rover, and Corus. Under his leadership, the Tata Group has become a global brand with a presence in over 100 countries.

Correction corner

Before he became the chairman of Tata Sons, Ratan Tata was involved in various initiatives to modernize the company and expand its global footprint.
